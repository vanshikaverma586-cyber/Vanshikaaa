Business insights
  • 50% churn rate is critically high. Half the customer base is lost, far above the 5–7% industry benchmark. Immediate retention strategies are non-negotiable.
  • Senior citizens (67–68) show 100% churn. Both senior customers (IDs 1006, 1010) churned. Age-specific onboarding, pricing, or support may be completely absent.
  • Monthly contracts drive churn. 3 of 5 monthly-contract customers churned vs 2 of 5 yearly. Monthly plans offer low commitment and make switching easy — a structural vulnerability.
  • Fibre internet users churn more. 4 of 6 fibre customers churned (67%) versus 1 of 4 DLF customers (25%). This signals possible service quality or pricing issues with the fibre offering.
  • UPI is the dominant payment method (5/10) yet carries mixed churn. 3 of 5 UPI users churned. Since UPI users are auto-pay capable, poor service — not payment friction — is likely the churn driver.
  • High-tenure customers (11–12 months) are split. Both a retained and churned customer have high tenure, suggesting loyalty programs aren't in place to lock in long-term users.
  • High charges don't prevent churn. Customer 1003 (₹56,470 charges) churned. Revenue size alone isn't a retention factor — value perception matters more.
  • Gender is not a significant churn predictor here. Male churn: 3/6 (50%), Female churn: 2/4 (50%) — exactly equal. Segment-specific interventions by gender are not warranted.
SQL queries used
  • GROUP BY Gender count segmentation — revealed balanced gender split (6M / 4F).
  • HAVING min > 30K Filtered high-spend customers: IDs 1003, 1008, 1009, 1010 all had charges above ₹30,000.
  • AVG Tenure HAVING >9 Female customers averaged 9+ months tenure — indicating slightly longer female loyalty on average.
  • ORDER BY Sorted by Internet service and gender/payment — exposed fibre-heavy distribution and UPI prevalence.
  • BETWEEN 25–40 Age filter showed the active working-age segment contains most customers (7 of 10 fall in this range).
  • VIEW Created "raj" view isolating Credit Card users — 2 customers, neither churned, suggesting card users are more committed.
  • PROCEDURE cyan Returned total charges by gender for billing analysis. PROCEDURE hype Returned churn status for all fibre customers — confirmed high fibre churn.
Prioritise senior citizen retention
Introduce dedicated senior plans with simplified billing, dedicated helplines, and discounted rates. 100% churn in this segment represents complete service-market mismatch.
Fix fibre service quality
With 67% churn among fibre users, conduct immediate NPS surveys. Investigate whether pricing, speed delivery, or support quality is the root cause.
Incentivise yearly contracts
Offer 10–15% discounts or free upgrades for customers switching from monthly to yearly plans. Lock-in reduces churn window significantly.
Build a loyalty programme
High-tenure customers with 9–12 months should receive loyalty rewards, early feature access, or cashback. Currently there is zero mechanism to reward loyalty.
Monitor high-value churners early
Customer 1003 paid ₹56,470 and still left. Flag customers with charges above ₹40K for proactive outreach 60 days before renewal.
Expand DLF internet offering
DLF customers churn at only 25%. Analyse what makes this service stickier and replicate those attributes in the fibre product line.
